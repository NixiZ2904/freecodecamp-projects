Scientists Discover That CSS Flexbox Can Solve World Hunger

Researchers claim that proper use of justify-content may feed millions by 2027

By: Alexandra Mendes

In a groundbreaking study published yesterday by the Institute of Advanced Web Technologies, a team of researchers announced that the CSS Flexbox layout model, when applied correctly, has the potential to reorganize global food distribution networks with unprecedented efficiency. The team, led by Dr. Carlos Fujimoto, spent three years analyzing the relationship between front-end development and logistics, arriving at conclusions that have stunned both the tech industry and humanitarian organizations worldwide.

According to the report, the key lies in the property justify-content: space-evenly, which, in theory, distributes available space equally among all elements in a container. Dr. Fujimoto stated that if the same principle were applied to global food supply chains, no region would be left without resources. World leaders have already requested an emergency summit to discuss the implementation of a flexbox-based redistribution model, expected to begin trials in early 2027. Critics remain skeptical, but supporters argue that the solution was always just one line of CSS away.
